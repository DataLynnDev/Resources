Amazon Data Scientist Mock Interview¶

The goal of this data challenge is to assess the candidate's skills in data manipulation, feature preprocessing and engineering, customer segmentation, and extracting business insights. The candidate will be asked to answer a series of questions using SQL and Python.

Data Tables¶

Customers:

  • Customer_Id: unique identifier for each customer
  • Age: customer age, ranging from 18 to 70
  • Gender: gender of the customer, F stands for female and M stands for male
  • Location: location of the customer, represented by city A to D
  • Tenure: number of years since the customer signed up
  • Avg_Session_Duration_min: the average time that each customer spend on each session in minutes
  • No_of_Purchases: the total number of purchases this customer has made

Products:

  • Product_Id: unique identifier for each product
  • Category: category of the product, including electronics, clothing, beauty and book
  • Brand: the brand of this product
  • Rating: average customer rating of the product

Customer_behavior:

  • Customer_Id: unique identifier for each customer
  • Product_Id: unique identifier for each product
  • Clicked: indicator of whether this customer has clicked on the link to this product, meaning that this customer viewed the product
  • Added_to_Cart: indicator of whether this customer added this product to their shopping cart
  • Purchased(TARGET VARIABLE): indicator of whether the customer purchased this product (0 means not purchased and 1 means purchased)

query_3 = """
WITH customer_spent AS (
    SELECT customers.Customer_Id AS c_Customer_Id, category, SUM(products.price) as total_spent
    FROM customers
    JOIN customer_behavior ON c_Customer_Id = customer_behavior.Customer_Id
    JOIN products ON customer_behavior.product_Id = products.product_Id
    WHERE purchased = 1
    GROUP BY c_Customer_Id, category
),

ranked_customers AS (
    SELECT customers.customer_id, location, no_of_purchases, category, total_spent,
           ROW_NUMBER() OVER(PARTITION BY location ORDER BY no_of_purchases DESC) as row_num
    FROM customers
    JOIN customer_spent ON customers.Customer_Id = customer_spent.c_Customer_Id
)
SELECT customer_id, location, no_of_purchases, category, total_spent
FROM ranked_customers
WHERE row_num <= 5;
"""

5. Business Problems¶

5.1 Suppose you've built a KNN model to predict whether a customer will purchase a product. How could you use the insights from this model to inform our business strategy? For instance, how could the model's predictions guide our product recommendation system, marketing efforts, or customer retention strategies?¶

The KNN model's predictions could be used to inform several aspects of the business strategy. For instance:

  • Product Recommendation: Products that are likely to be purchased (based on the model's predictions) could be highlighted or recommended to the customer.
  • Marketing: The business could target marketing or promotional efforts towards customers who are predicted to be on the fence about purchasing a product.
  • Customer Retention: The business could proactively reach out to customers who are predicted not to purchase, in order to understand their concerns and try to retain them.
