Step 2: Interpretation¶

2.1 Comment on sale_amount distribution

The sale_amount distribution reveals two distinct peaks, notably in the 100 and 400 price ranges. This suggests:

  • A significant concentration of transactions around these price points, possibly indicating popular product price points or frequent purchase combinations by customers.
  • The double-peaked nature might be a sign of two distinct customer groups or purchasing behaviors. One group may focus on budget items (around the 100 range) while another group might target more premium or bundled offerings (around the 400 range).

2.2 Comment on outliers

There are noticeable outliers in the higher price ranges. Given the context that orders can encompass multiple quantities of products, these high values are justified and don't necessarily indicate anomalies. It's crucial to understand the nature of these transactions. If they represent bulk orders or purchases of high-value items, they're expected. This contextual understanding allows us not to treat these as anomalies hastily, ensuring valuable transactions aren't mistakenly flagged or discarded.

2.3 Sale Amount vs. Return Status

From the observed data:

  • Transactions that haven't been returned (Return Status = 0) predominantly lie in the 200-300 price range. This could suggest that items within this price bracket are generally satisfactory to customers or perhaps are less risky purchases.
  • On the other hand, items that have been returned (Return Status = 1) show a concentration in the 700-800 price range. This higher price range for returns could be indicative of various factors. Customers might be more cautious or discerning with higher-priced items, leading to more returns. Alternatively, higher-value items might come with higher expectations, and any deviation might prompt a return.

General Insights:

  • The distinct price ranges associated with return statuses suggest varying customer behaviors or expectations at different price points. It may be beneficial for the business to dig deeper into the nature of products in these ranges and assess if specific product categories or attributes lead to more returns, especially in the higher price ranges.
  • A pattern where higher sale amounts are associated with a higher return rate could hint at potential issues in product quality, descriptions, or customer expectations at those price points. Addressing these issues can lead to improved customer satisfaction and reduced return rates.

Step 3: Identify Sellers with Multiple Product IDs and High Return Rate¶

We assume a "high return rate" is anything greater than 70%.

In [ ]:
%%sql
WITH ReturnRates AS (
    SELECT
        seller_id,
        product_id,
        SUM(CASE WHEN is_returned = 1 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS num_returns,
        COUNT(transaction_id) AS total_sales,
        (SUM(CASE WHEN is_returned = 1 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) * 1.0 / COUNT(transaction_id)) AS return_rate
    FROM Sales
    GROUP BY seller_id, product_id
    HAVING return_rate > 0.7
)

SELECT
    DISTINCT r.seller_id,
    r.product_id
FROM ReturnRates r
JOIN Sellers s ON r.seller_id = s.seller_id
WHERE strftime('%Y', 'now') - strftime('%Y', s.join_date) <= 1;

Probability and Statistical Analysis Questions

Scenario: To better understand the behavior of new sellers, you hypothesize that sellers who join the platform and immediately have high sales are more likely to be involved in fraudulent activities.

Task: Calculate the probability that a new seller (joined in the last month) will have sales in the top 10% of all sellers in their first month. Validate your hypothesis using the appropriate statistical tests on the Sellers table, making sure to consider potential over-fitting. Code the calculation to obtain the result.

Solution¶

Step 1: Identify New Sellers¶

Step 5: Statistical Test (One-Sample Z-test)¶

To validate the hypothesis, we can conduct a one-sample z-test. We'll compare the average sales of new sellers against the average sales of all sellers. If new sellers have a statistically significant higher average, it supports our hypothesis. We'll use a significance level of (alpha = 0.05).

In [ ]:
from scipy import stats
import numpy as np

# Calculate the population mean and standard deviation
population_mean = total_sales_per_seller.mean()
population_std = total_sales_per_seller.std()

# Calculate the sample mean
sample_mean = new_sellers_monthly_sales.mean()

# Calculate the z-statistic
z = (sample_mean - population_mean) / (population_std/np.sqrt(len(new_sellers_monthly_sales)))

# Calculate the p-value
p_value = 1 - stats.norm.cdf(z)

print(f"Z-statistic: {z:.2f}")
print(f"P-value: {p_value:.4f}")

Z-statistic: 13.69
P-value: 0.0000

Based on the results of the statistical test, the p-value is 0.0000, which is significantly less than the threshold value of 0.05. This allows us to reject the null hypothesis. Consequently, we can conclude that new sellers have a sales average that is statistically different from that of the general population of sellers. The evidence suggests that there is a significant difference in the sales performance between new sellers and the broader group of sellers.

Insights:¶

  1. Product Diversity: New sellers have a significantly smaller product range (234 unique products) compared to established sellers (1827 unique products). This difference indicates that new sellers are not diversifying their product offerings as much as they could.

  2. Average Customer Rating: Interestingly, new sellers (3.30 average rating) have a slightly higher average customer rating than established sellers (2.95 average rating). This suggests that while newer sellers are delivering a satisfactory customer experience, there's room for improvement.

  3. Return Rate: New sellers have a return rate of 53.60%, which is higher than the 49.86% of established sellers. While the difference isn't vast, it still indicates a potential issue with the product quality, mismatched product descriptions, or customer expectations from new sellers.

  4. Revenue Contribution: New sellers contributed $265,508.36, a fraction compared to the $2,715,009.51 from established sellers. This large gap suggests that new sellers are not tapping into the market's potential.

Recommendations:¶

  • Expand Product Portfolio: New sellers should be encouraged to expand their product offerings. Diversification can help them tap into different customer segments and improve revenue potential.

  • Feedback and Improvement: Even though new sellers have a decent rating, continuous feedback and iterative improvement can push this even higher. Regularly soliciting customer feedback and addressing common complaints will enhance the customer experience.

  • Addressing Return Rates: Given that new sellers have a slightly higher return rate, there's a need for interventions. Offering training sessions on product quality, packaging, and accurate product descriptions can reduce these return rates. Moreover, analyzing return reasons in-depth can offer more specific areas of improvement.

  • Targeted Marketing for New Sellers: Given the stark difference in revenue contributions, platform management should consider promoting new sellers more aggressively. This can be through "New Seller Spotlight" sections, dedicated advertising campaigns, or even time-limited discounts to encourage customers to buy from newer sellers.

  • Pricing Strategy Review: It might be worthwhile to conduct a thorough review of the pricing strategies adopted by new sellers. Ensuring that their prices are competitive can lead to higher sales. Offering dynamic pricing tools or market insights can assist sellers in this aspect.

  • Engagement with Established Sellers: Learning from the best is always a good strategy. Organize sessions where established sellers can share their experiences, strategies, and best practices with the newer sellers.

  • Platform Features & Tools: The platform can consider introducing features that highlight new product listings or trending products among new sellers. Additionally, offering analytics tools for sellers to understand customer behavior and preferences can be beneficial.

Incorporating these insights and recommendations will provide a holistic approach to addressing the revenue discrepancy and ensuring sustainable growth for both new and established sellers.
