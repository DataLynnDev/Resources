Step 2: SQL Query:¶

Here's the SQL query in theory:

SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT genre)
FROM Data_Info
WHERE region = 'North America'
AND listen_month = 'January 2023'
AND track_popularity > 85;

import sqlite3

# Establish a connection and cursor
conn = sqlite3.connect("spotify_db.sqlite3")
cur = conn.cursor()

# Convert dataframe to SQL table
df_data_info.to_sql("Data_Info", conn, if_exists="replace", index=False)

# Execute the SQL query
cur.execute("""
SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT genre)
FROM Data_Info
WHERE region = 'North America'
AND listen_month = 'January 2023'
AND track_popularity > 85;
""")

# Fetch and print the result
unique_genres_count = cur.fetchone()[0]
print(f"Number of unique genres with a track popularity score above 85 in North America in January 2023: {unique_genres_count}")

Step 3: Interpretation & Conclusion¶

From the results:

  1. Hip-hop was the genre with the highest average track popularity (about 61.88) in January, though it had fewer listen counts than some other genres.

  2. Jazz follows closely in terms of track popularity. However, it had a considerably higher listen count, making it quite a popular choice amongst European listeners in January.

  3. Electronic and Pop had lower track popularity than Hip-hop and Jazz, but their listen counts were higher. This could indicate that while individual tracks might not have been as popular, the genre as a whole saw more plays.

  4. Classical music had the lowest popularity amongst the top 5 genres and also a lower listen count.

Interpretation:

If a specific genre suddenly became very popular in Europe for February 2023:

  • If it's one of the top genres from January (like Hip-hop or Jazz), it can be inferred that the trend continued from the previous month.

  • If the suddenly popular genre for February is different from these top genres of January, then it's a significant shift in listener preference. For example, if Classical suddenly became the most popular genre in February, it would be a notable change.

To estimate the likelihood of this event based on January's data:

  • If the genre was already trending in January (like Hip-hop or Jazz), then its rise in February is not entirely surprising.

  • If it wasn't in the top genres in January, then something might have triggered its rise in February. Factors could be new album releases, concerts, cultural events, etc.

In summary, to estimate the likelihood of the event, one should look at both track popularity and listen count. If the genre's track popularity was already high in January, then it's more likely for that trend to continue into February. On the other hand, if a genre that wasn't as popular in January suddenly becomes a hit in February, external factors might be at play.
