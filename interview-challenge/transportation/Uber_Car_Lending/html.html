from sklearn.model_selection import train_test_split
from sklearn.ensemble import RandomForestClassifier

Question 3¶

Pricing the lending service is crucial for both car owners and potential drivers. Using available data, discuss a mechanism to decide on a lending price. Consider aspects like car model popularity, owner preferences, and potential driver demand.

Problem Solving Ideas and Tips:

  • The lending price can be influenced by both the supply (number of cars available of a particular model) and the demand (how popular a model is among potential drivers).
  • While the exact price can't be determined without a 'LendingPrice' column, you can derive an importance score based on various factors.
  • This score can then be used in various ways: as a reference point for bidding, as a base price with surcharges/discounts based on demand and supply, or as an input to a more sophisticated pricing algorithm.

Solution:

Step 1

1. Deriving an Importance Score for Each Car Model: To determine the "importance" or "value" of each car model, you can use the popularity of the model, owner rating, car age, and previous rentals.

2. Mechanism Suggestion:

Given the derived ImportanceScore:

  • Static Pricing:

    • Price cars based on their ImportanceScore, higher the score, higher the base lending price.
    • Consider operational costs. While the importance score provides a market-driven price, don't forget to factor in operational costs (like insurance, maintenance, Ubers commission) to ensure profitability.

  • Bidding System with Base Price:

    • Use the ImportanceScore to derive a base price for each car. Allow potential drivers to bid on the cars, but use this derived price as a minimum bidding threshold.

In the absence of actual lending price information, these strategies use derived metrics and available data to inform pricing decisions.

Here's an example of deriving the importance score:

# Assuming a CarOwners DataFrame
# Giving weights based on the perceived importance of each factor (these weights can be adjusted based on business insights)
weights = {
    'popularity': 0.3, # based on the number of each model
    'OwnerRating': 0.3,
    'CarAge': -0.1,  # Negative as older cars might be less valuable
    'PreviousRentals': 0.1
}

# Count the popularity of each car model
popularity = CarOwners['CarModel'].value_counts(normalize=True)
scale_factor = 1.2

# Calculate an importance score for each car
CarOwners['ImportanceScore'] = (
    popularity[CarOwners['CarModel']].values * weights['popularity'] +
    CarOwners['OwnerRating'] * weights['OwnerRating'] +
    (CarOwners['CarAge'] / CarOwners['CarAge'].max()) * weights['CarAge'] +
    CarOwners['PreviousRentals'] / CarOwners['PreviousRentals'].max() * weights['PreviousRentals']
) * scale_factor

# Display top 10 cars based on ImportanceScore
top_cars = CarOwners.sort_values(by='ImportanceScore', ascending=False).head(10)

Step 2

  1. Create a PricingScale based on PotentialDrivers attributes:
  • Driver Experience: Higher experience can get discounts due to less perceived risk.
  • Accident History: More accidents can lead to an added premium due to higher perceived risk.
  1. Based on this scale, a multiplier can be calculated for each potential driver, which can then be applied to the base price (calculated from the ImportanceScore of CarOwners) to get the final lending price for that specific driver-car combination.

Steps:

  1. Creating the Pricing Scale based on Driver Attributes:
  • Divide drivers into experience tiers (e.g., Newbie, Intermediate, Expert) based on driving experience years.
  • Calculate a risk factor based on accident history.
  1. Determining Lending Price:
  • For a specific driver, use the ImportanceScore from CarOwners to get the base price of the car they're interested in.
  • Apply the multiplier (based on the pricing scale) to get the final lending price.

# Sample model-specific base amounts
MODEL_BASE_AMOUNTS = {
    'Toyota Camry': 55,
    'Honda Civic': 52,
    'Ford Focus': 58,
    'Chevrolet Malibu': 60,
    'BMW 3 Series': 80,
    'Tesla Model 3': 85,
    'Audi A4': 88,
    'Mercedes C Class': 92,
    'Hyundai Elantra': 56
}

# Dividing drivers into experience tiers
bins = [0, 5, 15, np.inf]
labels = ['Newbie', 'Intermediate', 'Expert']
PotentialDrivers['ExperienceTier'] = pd.cut(PotentialDrivers['DrivingExperience'], bins=bins, labels=labels, right=False)

# Calculating risk factor based on accident history
PotentialDrivers['RiskFactor'] = PotentialDrivers['AccidentHistory'].apply(lambda x: 1.2 if x > 2 else (1.1 if x > 0 else 1.0))

# Creating the pricing scale
pricing_scale = {
    'Newbie': 1.1,
    'Intermediate': 1,
    'Expert': 0.95
}

PotentialDrivers['PriceMultiplier'] = PotentialDrivers['ExperienceTier'].map(pricing_scale).astype(float) * PotentialDrivers['RiskFactor']

# Step 2: Determining Lending Price

def get_lending_price(driver_id, car_model):
    base_amount_for_model = MODEL_BASE_AMOUNTS.get(car_model, 100)  # Default to 100 if model not in dictionary
    base_price = CarOwners[CarOwners['CarModel'] == car_model]['ImportanceScore'].iloc[0] * base_amount_for_model  # Convert importance score to a base price value
    multiplier = PotentialDrivers[PotentialDrivers['DriverID'] == driver_id]['PriceMultiplier'].iloc[0]

    return base_price * multiplier

# Example: Get lending price for driver with ID 10 interested in 'Toyota Camry'
price = get_lending_price(10, 'Toyota Camry')
print(f"The lending price for driver with ID 10 interested in 'Toyota Camry' is: {price:.2f}")

Question 4¶

Before launching this new service, it's essential to gauge its viability and potential risks. Discuss how you would assess the potential success of this initiative using the available data. Consider potential risks and how to do A/B test.

Problem Solving Ideas and Tips:

  1. Historical Data Analysis: Check if Uber has any historical data on similar services or pilot programs. Analyzing past failures or successes can give insights.
  2. Market Research: Before launching any service, it's essential to understand the demand. Market surveys can gauge potential interest.
  3. Risks Assessment: Enumerate potential risks from various angles: financial, reputational, operational, and legal.
  4. Simulations: Use the data to simulate the service's performance in different scenarios.
  5. Alternative to A/B Testing: Given that conventional A/B testing isn't ideal, consider phased roll-outs or pilot programs in limited regions.

Solution:

  1. Historical Data Analysis:

    • Use data from any similar services Uber might have offered in the past. For instance, check the success rate of car-sharing services or driver incentives.
    • Look at metrics like user adoption rate, frequency of service usage, and customer feedback.

  2. Market Research:

    • Conduct surveys among the current user base to gauge interest in such a service. For instance, ask car owners if they'd be willing to rent out their vehicles and under what conditions.
    • Analyze data from other players in the car rental space for trends and customer preferences.

  3. Risks Assessment:

    • Financial Risk: If the car gets damaged, who bears the repair cost? Consider insurance implications.
    • Reputational Risk: Bad experiences (like renting a malfunctioning car) can harm Uber's brand. Ensure thorough quality checks.
    • Operational Risk: Ensure a smooth logistics chain for handing over vehicles to drivers and returning them to owners.
    • Legal Risk: Different regions have varied regulations around car rentals. It's vital to ensure compliance.

  4. Simulations:

    • Use the CarOwners and PotentialDrivers datasets to simulate how often cars might be rented out, potential earnings, and associated costs. This can give a projected ROI for the service.

  5. Alternative to A/B Testing:

    • Pilot Program: Launch the service in a limited region. This tests the waters without a full commitment.
    • Phased Roll-out: Begin with a soft launch, introducing the service to a select group of users, and then gradually expand based on feedback and performance metrics.
    • Feedback Mechanism: Ensure there's a robust mechanism for users (both drivers and car owners) to provide feedback during the pilot or initial phase.

In conclusion, while the idea of renting out unused cars has potential, it's essential to approach it systematically. Data-driven insights, combined with market research and risk assessment, can guide the successful launch of this service.
